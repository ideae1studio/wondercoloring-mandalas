The Soul of This Mandala

Over 2,000 years ago, in India’s temples, Ganesha—the son of Shiva and Parvati—was invoked before any ritual, journey, or new beginning, with offerings of *modak* (rice sweets) and lotus flowers. Ganesha isn’t a distant god. He’s the seeker’s intimate friend, who removes inner and outer obstacles with wisdom, humor, and compassion. According to myth, he lost his human head, and Shiva gave him an elephant’s—symbol of ancestral memory, earthly intelligence, and gentle strength.

Place a small symbolic offering nearby: a flower, a sweet, or a written intention. Breathe and whisper: “May my path open with wisdom, joy, and purpose.” Don’t seek perfect colors. Ganesha loves sincere hearts, not perfect lines. If you make a mistake, laugh. He would too.
