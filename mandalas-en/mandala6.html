The Mandala of Tara’s Heart

Gentle music with Tibetan bells and Tara chants to activate active compassion and heart healing.

The Soul of This Mandala

Over 1,500 years ago, Tibetan monks told a sacred story: Avalokiteshvara, the Buddha of Compassion, wept at the world’s suffering. From his tears, Tara was born—a luminous goddess, swift as the wind, ready to act where others hesitated. Tara is no passive figure. She is the warrior mother of active compassion. In Vajrayana Buddhism, she’s invoked as “She Who Liberates from Fear” and “She Who Saves in the Right Moment.”

Place a hand over your heart. Breathe deeply and repeat softly: “May my compassion begin with me.” Choose one color for your tenderness, another for your strength. Let them intertwine in every petal. You don’t have to save the world today. You only need to allow yourself to be loved… even by you.
